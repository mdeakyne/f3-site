**Format:** Circle of Pain**Type:** Endurance / Core / Strength**Concept:** Ride the WaveThe SetupPAX form a tight circle, facing inward. Each PAX will hold the “worst” or hardest position of the current exercise (as called by the Q). The Q sets the tone and tempo — the **wave** starts with him.The Flow

1. **Q starts the wave:** Performs **1 rep** of the current exercise while everyone else holds the static position.
2. The wave moves **clockwise** — each PAX performs their 1 rep while others continue to hold.
3. Once it comes back to the Q, the Q performs **2 reps**.
4. The wave continues — each PAX now performs 2 reps when it’s their turn.
5. The count continues to climb each time the wave returns to the Q (3, 4, 5, etc.), until Q calls time or the set ends.

The Catch

- Everyone always holds the worst position while waiting.
- The goal is to “ride the wave” — stay strong through the hold, stay focused during your reps.
- When fatigue hits, it’s all about teamwork: everyone feels the same burn, together.
